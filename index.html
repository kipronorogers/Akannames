The Kingdom of GHANA

Amid the 9th and 11th Century C.E, in the west of Sahara lay a Kingdom laden with wealth and riches beyond belief. Occupying the present-day borders of Senegal, Mali and Mauritania, medieval Ghana was a gold mine.

The Kingdom of Ghana, the occupants were so rich that it is said that dogs wore golden collars, whereas colts dozed on opulent carpets!. The abundance of natural resources produced the world riches men coupled with trade and good system of governance making it a very wealthy empire of time.

Evidence of Ghana’s occupation dates back to the 4th century, but it was several hundred years later that it became established as a nation by the tribe of Soninke, whose leaders were credited with the early strengthening of the Wagudugu state and the expansion of the territories.

History of Akan Names

The Akan people of Ghana frequently name their children after the day of the week they were born and the order in which they were born. The “day names” have further meanings concerning the soul and character of the person.
Monday-born are Kwadwo or Kojo, Adjoa or Adwoa (meaning ‘associated with peace’); Tuesday-born are Kwabena or Kobi, Abena (meaning ‘associated with the ocean’); Wednesday-born Kwaku or Kweku, Akua (meaning ‘associated with spider/Ananse Thursday-born Yaw, Yaa ( meaning ‘associated with the earth’); Friday-born Kofi, Afia or Afua (meaning ‘associated with fertility’); Saturday-born Kwame, Ama (meaning ‘associated with God’).
